Understanding Debits & Credits

Debits (Dr) and Credits (Cr) are the two fundamental aspects of every financial transaction in the double-entry accounting system. They are used to record changes in the value of accounts.

The Core Rules:

Instead of thinking of "good" or "bad", think of debits and credits as simply "left" and "right" entries in an account. The key is to remember how they affect different types of accounts.

  • A "Debit" is an entry on the left side of an account.
  • A "Credit" is an entry on the right side of an account.

How They Affect Accounts (The DEA/LER Rule):

A simple way to remember the rules is with these two acronyms:

  • DEA: To increase Dividends (or Drawings), Expenses, and Assets, you Debit them.
  • LER: To increase Liabilities, Equity, and Revenue, you Credit them.

Conversely, to decrease any of these accounts, you do the opposite. For example, to decrease an Asset, you would Credit it.
