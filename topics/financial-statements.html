The Core Financial Statements

Financial statements are formal records of a business's financial activities. They provide a snapshot of a company's health and performance. The three primary statements are the Income Statement, the Balance Sheet, and the Statement of Cash Flows.

1. The Income Statement

Also known as the Profit and Loss (P&L) statement, it shows a company's financial performance over a specific period (e.g., a month, quarter, or year).

{Revenues} - {Expenses} ={Net Income (or Loss)}

2. The Balance Sheet

The Balance Sheet presents a snapshot of a company's financial position at a single point in time. It is a direct representation of the accounting equation.

{Assets} = {Liabilities} + \text{Equity}

Net income from the Income Statement increases the Equity on the Balance Sheet (through Retained Earnings).

3. The Statement of Cash Flows

This statement shows how cash has moved into and out of the company from three types of activities: operating, investing, and financing. It reconciles the beginning and ending cash balances over a period.
