The Journal Entry

The general journal is where business transactions are first recorded. Each individual recording is called a journal entry. It is the first step in the accounting cycle and acts as a chronological diary of all financial events.

Components of a Journal Entry:

  • Date: The date the transaction occurred.
  • Accounts Affected: At least two accounts are always affected (one debited, one credited).
  • Debits: The account(s) being debited are listed first, usually flush with the left margin.
  • Credits: The account(s) being credited are listed next and are typically indented.
  • Memo/Explanation: A brief description of the transaction.

Example:

Let's say a company purchases $500 of office supplies with cash on June 24, 2025.

  • Office Supplies (an Asset) increases. To increase an asset, you Debit it.
  • Cash (an Asset) decreases. To decrease an asset, you Credit it.
